# Keto Asian chicken with peanut coleslaw

Tired of humdrum chicken legs? Add an Asian flair to this keto chicken thigh dish. The meat is infused with a zesty tamari, garlic, and ginger marinade, then baked to crispy perfection. Served on a bed of coleslaw with a spicy peanut sauce, and garnished with avocado and lime, this meal is an exotic trip without leaving home.

**Source:** [dietdoctor.com](https://www.dietdoctor.com/recipes/keto-asian-chicken-with-peanut-coleslaw)

## Macros (per serving)
- **Calories:** 987 kcal
- **Protein:** 44 g
- **Fat:** 84 g
- **Net carbs:** 4 g (total 10g − fiber 6g)

## Time
- Prep: 20 min
- Cook: 30 min
- Total: 50 min
- Servings: 4

## Ingredients
- 2 lbs chicken thighs (bone-in with skin)
- 1 1/3 tbsp ginger garlic paste
- 3 tbsp tamari soy sauce
- 1 tbsp coconut oil
- 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
- 8 oz. red cabbage
- 6 oz. bean sprouts
- 1/2 + 1 tbsp (optional) red chili pepper
- 2 oz. carrots
- 1 oz. + 2 tbsp (optional) fresh cilantro
- 2 tbsp peanut butter
- 3/4 cup mayonnaise
- 1/2 tbsp sriracha sauce
- 1/4 tsp sesame oil
- 1 avocado
- 1 lime (optional)

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. In a small bowl, whisk together the marinade ingredients. Place the chicken in a ziplock bag, and add the marinade to completely coat the chicken. Set aside for 10 minutes at room temperature.
3. Place the chicken thighs, skin-side up on a broiler or baking pan. Bake for 30-40 minutes depending on the size of the thighs, or until the skin is crispy and golden brown and the internal temperature is 165°F (74°C). Set aside to cool.
4. Add the coleslaw ingredients to a large bowl. In a smaller bowl, whisk together the dressing ingredients. Pour it over the coleslaw, and give it a good mix.
5. Divide the coleslaw into individual serving bowls, or leave in the current bowl. Garnish with the chicken, diced avocado, chili, and a lime wedge. Another option: Serve the chicken thighs with the coleslaw on the side.
