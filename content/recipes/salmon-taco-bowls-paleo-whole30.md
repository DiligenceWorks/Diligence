# Salmon Taco Bowls (Paleo, Whole30)

These Salmon Taco Bowls are easy, healthy and packed with flavor and texture. You can customize them however you want and they&#39;re naturally gluten-free, dairy-free, low carb, keto, Paleo and Whole30 friendly!

**Source:** [paleoglutenfreeguy.com](https://paleoglutenfreeguy.com/salmon-taco-bowls-paleo-whole30/)

## Macros (per serving)
- **Calories:** 736 kcal
- **Protein:** 35 g
- **Fat:** 62 g
- **Net carbs:** 5 g (total 7g − fiber 2g)

## Time
- Prep: 15 min
- Cook: 20 min
- Total: 35 min
- Servings: 4

## Ingredients
- 4 (6 oz.) salmon fillets (with or without skin, room temperature)
- 2 teaspoons chili powder
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- ¾ teaspoon fine sea salt
- ½ teaspoon chipotle flakes or ground chipotle powder
- ½ teaspoon ground cayenne powder
- 2 tablespoons avocado oil, olive oil or melted coconut oil
- 1 cup avocado oil mayonnaise
- 1 cup salsa
- avocado oil spray or olive oil spray
- Veggies and toppings  ((see note below for ideas))

## Instructions
1. Heat oven to 425°.
2. Line a baking sheet with foil or parchment paper, then spray it with a light coating of olive or avocado oil spray.
3. Pat the salmon filets dry with paper towels and place them on the prepared baking sheet. If the fillets have their skin on and you don&#39;t want it, leave the skin on for now. It&#39;s much easier to remove after the salmon is cooked.
4. In a small bowl, stir together the chili powder, cumin, oregano, coriander, garlic powder, onion powder salt, chipotle flakes/powder and cayenne powder. Then stir in the avocado oil or olive oil.
5. Evenly coat the salmon fillets with the spice paste.
6. Bake the salmon for 8-10 minutes (if using a probe thermometer, insert it into the thickest fillet now), until the internal temperature reaches 145°. Remove the salmon from the oven and let cool.
7. Meanwhile, prep your veggies and toppings: shred cabbage, chop cilantro, etc.
8. In a medium bowl, stir or whisk together the mayonnaise and salsa. Taste and add more mayo or salsa, depending on your preference, if desired.
9. Evenly distribute the veggies to each bowl. Add one fillet per bowl (peeling away the skin, if desired). Drizzle or spoon over the sauce and dig in!
